about

As both a teacher and a student I have always approached language learning, programming or otherwise, as a kind of game. The pleasure that comes from untangling a tricky Latin sentence and the enjoyment of writing an elegant, recursive JavaScript function satisfy the same puzzle-solving impulse. The immersive Software Engineering course at General Assembly appealed to me as someone who enjoys learning new things at a pace and savours the opportunity to unpick challenging problems. Since starting the course I have also discovered a more practical side to myself, one which likes to tinker with broken objects or break things in order to fix them. Over the course of four main assignments, I have developed the stamina to build a project from the ground up and the ability to approach problems from several angles in order to arrive at the most efficient solution. Having worked as a college lecturer and a private tutor, I am now looking to find a supportive learning environment in which I can continue to learn from others and in turn pass on knowledge of my own.
