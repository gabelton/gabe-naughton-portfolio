about


As both a teacher and a student I have always approached language learning, programming or otherwise, as a kind of game. The pleasure that comes from untangling a tricky Latin sentence and the enjoyment of writing an elegant, recursive JavaScript function satisfy the same puzzle-solving impulse.

The immersive Software Engineering course at General Assembly appealed to me as someone who enjoys learning new things at a pace and savours the opportunity to unpick challenging problems. Since starting the course I have also discovered a more practical side to myself, one which likes to tinker with broken objects or break things in order to fix them. Over the course of four main assignments, I have developed the stamina to build a project from the ground up and the ability to approach problems from several angles in order to arrive at the most efficient solution.

Having worked as a college lecturer and a private tutor, I am now looking to find a supportive learning environment in which I can continue to learn from others and in turn pass on knowledge of my own.

current


For my final project on the General Assembly Software Engineering Immersive I created a full stack art events app called Canvas. For this I used Python, Flask, and Pony on the backend, alongside React Js on the frontend. Building on this, I am now working on a blogging app as I follow along with Miguel Grinberg's Python-Flask Mega Tutorial.

In my capacity as teaching assistant at General Assembly I have also recently built my first database with Django, a new addition to the course which I had to learn in order to help teach the current cohort.

In my remaining time I try to stay sharp by completing challenges on Codewars, often trying to refactor my solutions or translate from Python into JavaScript and vice versa.
